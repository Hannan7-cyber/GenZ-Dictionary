Gen Z Dictionary

Learn the latest slang and phrases used by Gen Z!

Dictionary

  • Sigma: Good.
  • Skibidi: Bad.
  • Aesthetic: A particular vibe or style.
  • Bet: Cool, alright, agreed.
  • Lit: Amazing, excellent, or exciting.
  • Fam: Close friends, group of friends.
  • No Cap: No lie, being truthful.
  • Slay: To do something excellently, or to look great.
  • Flex: To show off, often used for showing off wealth, looks, or achievements.
  • Sus: Suspicious, shady, or untrustworthy.
  • Chill: To relax or calm down. Also used as a greeting (e.g., "What's chill?").
  • Cap: A lie or falsehood. "No cap" means truth, and "cap" means a lie.
  • Vibe: The atmosphere or feeling someone or something gives off.
  • Drip: Refers to someone’s stylish clothing or accessories, often expensive or trendy.
  • FOMO: Fear of missing out, the anxiety about missing an event or experience.
  • Ghosting: The act of suddenly cutting off communication with someone, especially in a dating context.
  • Shade: Subtle, often negative, comments aimed at someone, typically in a sly or indirect way.
  • Woke: Being socially aware, particularly around social justice issues.
  • Bussin: Really good, amazing, or delicious, especially when talking about food.
  • Clout: Influence, power, or social status, often used in reference to social media.
  • Viral: Something that becomes extremely popular or widely shared on the internet.
  • Lowkey: Subtly or secretly; not openly displayed.
  • Highkey: The opposite of lowkey; openly, obviously, or honestly.
  • Cringe: Something awkward, embarrassing, or socially uncomfortable.
  • Savage: Doing something bold, fearless, or ruthless, often with no regard for others' opinions.
  • Tea: Gossip or the latest news, especially when it's juicy or scandalous.
  • Shook: Shocked, surprised, or shaken up by something.
  • Yeet: To throw something with force, or an exclamation of excitement or approval.
  • Lit: Exciting, fun, or awesome. A great time or event.
  • Stan: To strongly support or be a big fan of someone or something.
  • Bae: A term of endearment for a significant other (before anyone else).
  • Noob: A newbie or someone who is inexperienced in a particular activity or game.
  • Squad: A group of close friends or companions.
  • Flex: To show off or brag, often about material possessions or achievements.
